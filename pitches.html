Project re-Pitched: Barbie sales and population

How relevant is the 55-year-old Barbie?

Barbie, marketed to children that are between age 3 and 9, was first introduced by Mattel in 1959 and sold for $3. In the first year, 300,000 Barbie dolls were sold. The company went public in 1960. But in recent years, sales of Barbie (Mattel's biggest product line) has decreased. Using data from US census bureau and information from SEC filings (starting 1994), I would like to see if Barbie doll sales decline corresponds with the number of girls ages 3 to 9.
